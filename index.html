Valley Clear — Marketing Analytics

Turning flyer routes and door-to-door leads into a real cost-per-acquisition model.

15.5× ROI on the best-performing channel — measured from 49 real Arizona jobs, not a sample dataset.

I run Valley Clear, a window & solar-panel cleaning business, across the Phoenix metro area. This year I started tracking every job's lead source and built a Splunk dashboard to find out which marketing channel was actually worth the spend — then used the same discipline to model what a new channel mix could look like next quarter.

Where each assumption came from

V3 flyer
$421 for 5,000 double-sided flyers. Conversion set at 0.6% — matched to this year's real Fall-flyer conversion rate (15 jobs ÷ 2,500 flyers), not an optimistic guess.
Meta ad
$75 CPA against a $225 average ticket — sits inside the real 2026 industry benchmark range for home-services Meta lead gen ($25–$75 CPA).
d2d
My own canvassing, done after completed jobs. $0 direct cost, sized at 15% of projected volume.
Customer referral
Held at the real historical rate — about one referral a month — rather than assumed to grow.

How this was built

Job records started as a messy spreadsheet — price, lead source, and notes all mixed into unstructured text. I cleaned and restructured that into fields Splunk could query, then wrote the SPL behind every stat, table, and chart on this page myself: channel-level stats aggregations, eval-based CPA/ROI/profit calculations, and a monthly timechart for the trend view.
